Hello, I am **Justus Meyer zu Bexten**. Since my last introduction looked more like a corporate CV, here is a more compact non-exhaustive insight.

### My Career
After military service in Mittenwald and a Bachelor's degree in Computer Science in Jena, I moved to Leipzig for a Master's in Data Science (interrupted by a semester abroad in Crete). 
Parallel to this, I was working continuously: As a software developer at [Salesforce](https://www.salesforce.com/de/) and in research at the [Jena Computer Vision Group](https://inf-cv.uni-jena.de/home/staff/former-students/) as well as the [Max Planck Institute for Human Cognitive and Brain Sciences](https://www.cbs.mpg.de/methods-and-development-groups/neural-data-science-and-statistical-computing).
Now, I am working on my own scientific endeavours through my PhD at [ScaDS.AI](https://scads.ai/), in continued collaboration with the [MPI-CBS](https://www.cbs.mpg.de/methods-and-development-groups/neural-data-science-and-statistical-computing).

My focus lies in Deep Learning, particularly on Transformers for time series, language, and neuroscience. I describe myself as an optimistic skeptic: while I am often exhausted by current promises, I firmly believe in the potential of the method for data that eludes classical modeling – such as in medicine.


### My Interests

I firmly believe in a life outside of work. You can find me weekly on a run to Lake Cospuden, climbing, or swimming. Furthermore, the international community is close to my heart: cultural encounters provide a benchmark for one's own world and make hostility more difficult. While I now tend to stay in the background of international student associations like [WILMA e.V.](https://www.wilma.uni-leipzig.de/en/home/) or [ESN Jena](https://jena.esn-germany.de), I am keeping my eyes open for new ways of engagement.
